Frequently Asked Questions

How do I choose a puppy?

A lot of things needs to be considered while getting a new puppy as a family pet. Puppies will need a lot of time for training, grooming and socializing. Therefore, the amount of time that can be devoted each day for puppy's requirement is to be considered the most. For example, a golden retriever or a long haired German Shepard will require ample time just for grooming, whereas a min-pin or a JRT might require zero grooming. Similarly, the space we have should also be considered, generally speaking large breeds like Great Dane or Saint Bernard will require more space.


Considering other things, it is also a matter of personal preference. Some people tend to like one breed to the other. There is also a belief that certain dog breeds are friendlier and certain breeds are more aggressive. Which we think is not true. Any dog of any breed can be trained to be friendly and gentle, and almost all dogs can also be trained to show aggression.
However, in a litter of puppies there are different personalities and choosing the puppy with right personality drive and temperament can be very beneficial in the long run. There are methods that can be used as puppy personality test.

When do I start Training my Puppy?

Researches have shown us that puppy training and conditioning can start as early as 10/12 days. Breeders play different sounds near puppies to condition them to a noisy world that they are going to be in later. As soon as the pups start crawling they instinctively start to crawl outside their sleeping area to pee and poo, this instinctive behavior can be used to potty-train puppies and to get them used to the surfaces they are allowed to relieve themselves.
Once the puppy start walking they should learn to follow their humans around, early training has big impact in a dog's life that lasts very long. With that said, our training should being the very day we bring our puppy in our homes and first things that the puppy needs to learn are crate training, potty training, learning to follow humans etc.

My dog is X years old, can we still train him?

Depending upon the size and breed, some dogs develop early, for example a pom puppy will start acting like an adult when it's around 9 months old whereas a saint puppy will only start acting like an adult around 3 years. However, puppy that have not grown into adults are a lot easier to train than they are after dogs reach adulthood. With that said, all dogs can still learn, no matter how old they are. Just that if they have developed a positive attitude towards training while they are young, things are a lot easier.

What do i feed my puppy?

There are a lot of commercial dog foods available in the market, but when it comes to feeding we always recommend natural raw diet. We do have a lot of videos about how and why should we feed dogs the way we do in our YouTube and Facebook channel. Please go through those videos to find out more.
